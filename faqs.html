New Patient Visit
New Patient Packets and any other requested medical records must be received no later than 48 hours prior to your appointment. Arrive 30 minutes prior to scheduled time.
This time is used to:

  • Verify address and insurance information
  • Review privacy and appointment policies
  • Review medication list
  • Verify address and insurance information
  • Review privacy and appointment policies
  • Review medication list
  • Record changes that may have occurred since completing the New Patient Packet
  • Fill out patient check in sheet (relevant health information)
  • Check vitals (blood pressure, heart rate, weight, oxygen saturation level) During Appointment
  • History and physical exam
  • Additional tests may be ordered (urine drug test, blood tests, overnight sleep study, imaging, and other tests necessary to provide an accurate diagnosis or ensure safety when treatments are prescribed)
  • Medication is not prescribed on the first visit.

*Strongly recommended you bring all medications you take in the original container.

Your health information including medical appointments are confidential and protected by Federal Law.

If you are having a life threatening emergency call 911.
If you are in stable condition but need urgent medical attention you must go to a local Urgent Care Center or Emergency Room.
If you are stable and have questions or concerns about your care after business hours you can send a message via the patient portal or call and leave a message on voicemail. Using the patient portal is the fastest and most efficient way to communicate with your provider. Your message goes straight to the providers computer and these messages are checked throughout the work day.

Frequency of appointments depends on complexity of case and prescribed therapies.

Urine Drug Screens are performed per providers discretion and without warning.

Safety is our top priority at Pain GPS Clinic.
Pain management with opioids may be complicated and challenging. Providers need to know if patients can follow the treatment plan. Patients need to know the potential risks of opioids to themselves and others (children, animals, community) as well as the expectations to minimize those risks. Pain GPS Clinic uses a Treatment Agreement and a Material Risk Notice to make sure communication is clear and the patient understands.

What medications does the agreement include? We will review each paragraph with you.
What risks are involved with taking these medications? A Material Risk Notice is also reviewed, questions and concerns are answered.
How does the agreement affect emergency care? Treatment Agreements should not affect your ability to receive medical attention needed in the ER. Pain GPS Clinic recommends you share exactly what you take, how often, and why and the clinics contact information.
What if I fail to follow the agreement? Failing to comply with the contract may put your health at risk depending on the type of violation. Pain GPS Clinic will not prescribe narcotics if the Treatment Agreement is not honored. If this happens you will be tapered off of your opioids in a manner that is consistent with Stanfords University School of Medicine's Chronic Opioid Taper guidelines.

Safety is our top priority at Pain GPS Clinic. Pain GPS Clinic's policy is not to prescribe on initial visit.
