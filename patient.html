Your First Visit

New Patient Visit

New Patient Visit Before the appointment turn a completed New Patient Packet and any other requested medical records.
Arrive 30 minutes prior to your 1st scheduled appointment. This time is used to:

  • Verify address and insurance information
  • Review privacy and appointment policies.
  • Review medication list
  • Record any changes that may have occurred since completing the New Patient Packet or since your last appointment
  • Collecting information regarding sleep habits, exercise, alcohol and tobacco intake and any new treatments you have tried recently
  • Check vitals (blood pressure, heart rate, weight, oxygen saturation level)

During Appointment

  • The provider will ask more detailed questions about your history and physical exam. You may be asked to change into a gown prior to the exam. You may ask for a second person in the room at any time.
  • The physician may order additional tests, such as blood tests, overnight sleep study, special imaging, and other tests necessary to provide an accurate diagnosis or ensure your safety when treatments are prescribed.
  • Medication is not prescribed on the first visit.

No Show Policy

"No Show" means you missed your appointment without canceling 24 hours prior. All patients who do not show up to their appointment will be sent an initially warning letter. Following this warning, if a second "no show"you will be charged $50 and possibly be discharged from care and referred back to the primary care provider.

Telehealth Appointment

A telehealth appointment is done over a computer or smartphone where you can be seen and heard by your provider. You must complete the electronic check-in questionnaire prior to your Telehealth appointment. For the most effective use of the appointment time find a quiet place for the video call. Contact us 24 hours in advance at 541-246-3400 if you must cancel or reschedule the appointment.
